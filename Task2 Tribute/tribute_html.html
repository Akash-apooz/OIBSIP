Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true."

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was not just a name, he was a story of dreams, of hope, of science, and love for the country. Born in a small town Rameswaram in 1931, his life was not easy. But from the very beginning, he had a mind that asked questions and a heart that believed in possibilities.

He became a scientist and worked at ISRO and DRDO. He was a big reason behind India’s missile development. The Agni, the Prithvi, those names you hear – he was behind them. That’s why he was called the Missile Man. Not because he built weapons, but because he built confidence in a developing nation.

He also became the President of India, from 2002 to 2007. He was loved by students, teachers, and common people. He used to meet students often and answer their questions with a smile. He believed that youth were the future and used every opportunity to inspire them.

He wrote books too. Wings of Fire is his autobiography – a must read. He also wrote Ignited Minds and India 2020. His words felt like he was talking to you directly, not as a scientist or president, but like a friend.

Even when he got old, he never stopped working. He kept going to schools, colleges, giving speeches. He passed away while doing what he loved – talking to students. That’s how dedicated he was.

His life tells us – no matter where you come from, no dream is too big. He made science cool for kids. He showed what a true leader looks like. He will always remain in our hearts.
